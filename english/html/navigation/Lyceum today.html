REPORT
on the results of work in the 2020/2021 academic year and tasks for the 2021/2022 academic year
(implementation of the lyceum development strategy for 2020-2026)
Approved at the meeting of the labor collective
Protocol of 25 June 2021 №5
The organization of the educational process of Krasnograd Lyceum № 2 Krasnograd City Council of Kharkiv region is based on the Constitution of Ukraine, Laws of Ukraine "On Education", "On Complete General Secondary Education", "On Child Protection", "On Labor Protection", State Standards of Primary, Basic and complete general secondary school, normative documents of the Ministry of Education and Science of Ukraine, other normative acts and is conducted in accordance with the charter of the institution.
The main documents for the organization and conduct of educational activities in the institution are its charter, annual curriculum of Krasnograd Lyceum № 2 for the current school year, prepared in accordance with current legislation, while maintaining the development strategy of the institution for 202-2026, taking into account the wishes of parents and pedagogical staff, approved by the pedagogical council of the institution, and the annual work plan of Krasnograd Lyceum № 2 for the current academic year, approved by the pedagogical council.
Taking into account the network of classes, wishes of parents and students, staffing of the institution, in the 2020/2021 academic year specialized training was carried out as follows: 10th grade in profile subjects Ukrainian language and literature, 11th grade in profile subjects Ukrainian language and literature
In order for students to acquire life skills, the administration of the institution, the methodical council of the lyceum created a system of studying elective courses and elective courses in grades 5-11. In particular, the elective course "Lessons on Environmental Protection" is introduced for students of 5-6 grades, the elective course "Financial Culture" for the 7th grade students, and the elective course "Applied Finance" for the 8th grade students. For the formation of ethical culture of students of 5th - 6th grades an additional subject "Ethics" is introduced and for students of 8th - 9th grades to acquire knowledge about geography and historical past of the small homeland - an elective course "Kharkiv Studies". To develop the skills of stylistically competent oral and written speech and the development of communicative competence in 10 - 11 A classes introduced an optional course "Stylistics of the Ukrainian language" and an elective course "Masterpieces of modernism: study of Ukrainian and foreign literature in artistic context" to promote a healthy lifestyle life and prevention of HIV / AIDS - optional course "Forming a healthy lifestyle". For the formation of civic consciousness of 11B class students, an elective course "Formation of Ukrainian statehood" was introduced. Taking into account the professional orientation of high school students and taking into account the wishes of the parent community, additional time is devoted to the study of mathematics, history and biology.
The development of physical abilities, in addition to the work carried out by physical education teachers, was provided by the cooperation of the lyceum with Krasnograd CYSS. The basketball section and the sports dance section worked on the basis of the institution.
The Methodical Council of the Lyceum analyzed the joint work of the institution with out-of-school institutions of the district and recognized the most effective cooperation with Krasnograd CYSS and Krasnograd RCYUT in comparison with other areas of extracurricular activities because:
- close cooperation of specialists of these out-of-school establishments with teachers is provided
- a system of publishing students' achievements and holding creative exhibitions has been created
- it is practiced to involve students with special needs in creative work
- the level of cooperation of club leaders with parents has increased
In order to implement the above tasks, the methodological council reviewed, based on the diagnosis of self-assessment of teachers and the assessment of the administration, the level of professionalism of teachers and planned various forms of methodological work with staff to ensure the required level of knowledge, skills and abilities.
Special attention is paid to first graders. In accordance with the requirements of the concept of the New Ukrainian School, professional training of subject teachers and primary school teachers who worked in grades 1-2 was conducted, classrooms for first-graders were equipped. In addition, last school year the network of inclusive classes in the institution was increased (1A, 2B, 3B, 4B, 5A, 9A). There were 6 such classes (for comparison - last year there were 4). An extended day group also worked to meet the needs of first-graders. The presence of a game room made it possible to effectively organize the work of the group for an extended day.
At the beginning of the 2020/2021 school year, the institution had 508 students, which is 10 children less than in the previous year. 21 classes with an average capacity of 24 students have been completed. The study covers 100% of school-age children from 6 to 18 years of age living in the service area assigned to the institution.
During the previous school year, the teaching staff worked out a system of operational control of students' attendance and took measures against students who do not visit it without good reason, involving the Prevention Council, juvenile police, children's service Krasngrad City Council and other services. work with minors.
In the 2020/2021 academic year, first-, second-, and third-graders did not pass the state final attestation due to the epidemic situation in the country. However, 11th-graders were able to take an external independent assessment to enter higher education. In general, they showed an average and sufficient level of knowledge, although there are students who have low scores according to the results of the EIT and did not pass the threshold "passed / failed". As external independent evaluation, as a form of control over the competencies acquired in a general secondary education institution, is defined by the Ministry of Education and Science of Ukraine as a promising form of work, the current academic year will summarize the best experience of preparation direction to teachers of Ukrainian language and literature, history, mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, geography, working in 10 - 11 classes.
- 1 student was awarded the silver medal "For Achievements in Education" (last year they were not awarded);
- 4 9th grade students received certificates of basic general secondary education of a special type;
- 32 students per student were awarded letters of commendation (9.6%);
- 157 male and female students studied at the average level (42%);
- 176 male and female students (47%) studied at a sufficient level;
- 3 students who, according to the results of the annual assessment in some subjects had grades of entry level.
Analysis of the educational process in the institution showed that most students are socially active, have a clear civic position, respect the state and state symbols, respect elders, believe in the need for learning and self-development, although the main problem in the student environment is respect for others, self-esteem, respect to parents and social values. Therefore, the main directions of the educational process was patriotic education, the formation of values ​​to oneself, to life, to others, to the state, its symbols, laws and social order. The teaching staff has ensured the proper state of work regarding the implementation of the above areas of education and will continue to look for effective ways to organize the educational process this school year. Parents will be involved in the educational work as participants in the educational process, all services of the local community that work with children or adolescents, individual public organizations with their consent.
A separate area of ​​educational work this school year will be work to preserve the lives and health of students. In addition to the efforts of the teaching staff, it is planned to involve the civil service for emergencies in the Krasnograd district, representatives of the juvenile police, representatives of the Red Cross.
Active work in the 2020/2021 academic year was carried out on the use of health-preserving and STEAM technologies, elements of distance learning, forms of individual and inclusive learning in the system of organization of the educational process. This work included the following areas: nutrition, physical culture and health activities, mental and physical support of children in need, correctional and developmental work, and so on. At the same time, work was carried out to create a comfortable environment for children to stay in the institution and on the territory, which included the current renovation and modernization of classrooms, corridors and recreation.
In the 2020/2021 academic year, the institution enrolled 13 students with special needs, for whom the institution provides individual and inclusive forms of education. schedule, identified teachers and teaching assistants with appropriate qualifications and provided a quality educational process during the school year.
Last school year, quality work was carried out to organize the training of teachers. During the school year, 10 pedagogical councils were held, which, among other things, analyzed the shortcomings in the work of individual teachers and identified ways to overcome them. To enhance the professional orientation of the institution there were 5 school methodological associations, which included all teachers of the lyceum, their meetings developed ways to improve professional activities, studied regulations and identified ways to apply it. The long-term training plan was implemented properly. Members of the methodical council and the lyceum council determined perspective directions of the educational process development.
The work plan of the lyceum for the 2020/2021 academic year provided for systematic control and analytical activities of the administration, which was not fully implemented in connection with the KOVID-19 pandemic, but it ensured proper effectiveness in the system of academic achievement. An effective system of control over the execution of orders has been created.
The administration of the lyceum analyzed the work with parents and citizens who apply to the administration of the institution on various issues. It is established that the most effective form of cooperation between parents and teachers and class teachers is individual work in the presence of the child or without it and the involvement of the parent community in educational work or the development of cognitive abilities of students.component and with the maximum compliance with the norms of the Sanitary Regulations To improve the preservation of students' vision in all classrooms and classrooms installed modern lighting fixtures based on energy-saving technologies. New lighting fixtures are also installed in the corridors, recreation, gym. The entire scope of work on replacing windows with energy-saving ones has been completed. Measures are being taken to adjust the height of school desks to the height of students. To avoid injuring students in all classrooms and classrooms, as well as in public areas, the front door has been replaced with lighter and aesthetically pleasing. Purchased and installed desks and cabinets in the Ukrainian language classroom. Pupils of 1st, 2nd and 3rd grades also have new individual desks and other equipment, according to the concept of the New Ukrainian School. An emergency police call button has been installed.
The main value for the state, parents and teaching staff of the lyceum is not only the development of mental abilities, skills, aesthetic tastes of students, but also providing comfortable conditions for their stay in the institution. To this end, in classrooms, classrooms, corridors and recreation in June - July, current repairs were carried out taking into account the aesthetic
To avoid viral diseases and to determine the state of health of each student, the utility company "Center of Primary Medicine! in-depth medical examinations of students are held annually until September 1. The results of the reviews enable physical education teachers and class teachers to respond quickly to counteract the exacerbation of chronic diseases and build an educational process for individual children, taking into account the disease.
An important part of the systematic work of the institution is the organization of student nutrition, as it is one of the main components of health technologies that are practiced during the school year. For high-quality cooking, creating a comfortable environment in the place of its consumption, compliance with HACCP standards in the institution overhauled the food unit and dining room with the installation of modern technological equipment. In the long run, this will make it possible to more effectively meet the individual needs of students.
The work of the school food unit is designed to provide free hot meals for students in grades 1-4, students from low-income families, orphans and students in care, students with special needs. Hot meals are also organized for GPA students, all interested students by prior arrangement. In addition, employees of the food unit produce daily buffet products, which are sold during breaks. With the beginning of the new school year, class teachers organize hot meals for students in the class in agreement with the class parent committee.
The 2021/2022 school year will be the year of nutrition organization in accordance with the Sanitary Regulations and the new regulatory framework, which should ensure the preservation of students' health and avoid chronic diseases in some of them.
In order to avoid the purchase of low-quality products, employees of the food unit receive food and ingredients only on the basis of tenders conducted by the Department of Education and the mandatory list of supporting documents.
In order to restore the health and stimulate the cognitive abilities of students after the end of the school year in the institution begins work school recreation camp with a day stay "Prometheus", where they are enrolled on the personal applications of parents. Pupils of the camp are provided with daily hot meals, classrooms are converted into games, which allows them to engage in group or extracurricular activities. Annual rest in the school camp is provided for 70 percent of the contingent of lyceum students. However, last school year the pandemic did not allow to start work in the camp.
In 2020/2021 academic year training had its own characteristics associated with the coronavirus pandemic KOVID 19. In order to preserve their health, the institution has created conditions for hygiene, all participants in the educational process, distance while moving indoors, temperature screening, hand disinfection, premises.

HOW EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS WILL WORK
AT THE "YELLOW" LEVEL OF EPID-HAZARD OF COVID-19
On September 23, Ukraine moved to the "yellow" level of epidemic danger of the spread of COVID-19. In this regard, the Government has updated the quarantine conditions, in particular regarding the operation of educational institutions.
At the "yellow" level, applicants will be allowed to visit educational institutions provided that at least 80% of the employees of the institution have one of the required documents, the list of which is published here. If the educational institution has less than 80% of employees who have one of the required documents, training should continue at a distance. In addition, at the "yellow" level of epidemic danger is prohibited:
- visits to educational institutions by applicants when more than 50% of educational institutions and staff of educational institutions are in self-isolation due to contact with a patient with a confirmed case of COVID-19;
- holding mass events (performances, holidays, concerts) in educational institutions with the participation of applicants from more than one group (class) and in the presence of spectators.

cybersecurity
What is "cybersecurity"? This area is concerned with protecting digital information, operating systems, computer networks, servers, databases, public and private institutions from unauthorized interference. The Law of Ukraine “On Basic Principles of Ensuring Cyber ​​Security of Ukraine” (Vidomosti Verkhovnoi Rady (VVR), 2017, № 45, p. 403) provides the following definition: “Cybersecurity is the protection of vital interests of man and citizen, society and the state , which ensures the sustainable development of the information society and digital communication environment, timely detection, prevention and neutralization of real and potential threats to Ukraine's national security in cyberspace.
Cyberspace is an environment (virtual space) that provides opportunities for communication and / or social relations, resulting from the operation of compatible (connected) communication systems and the provision of electronic communications using the Internet and / or other global data networks
Cyberattack - directed (intentional) actions in cyberspace, which are carried out by means of electronic communications (including information and communication technologies, software, software and hardware, other technical and technological means and equipment) and aimed at achieving one or a combination of the following objectives: integrity, availability of electronic information resources that are processed (transmitted, stored) in communication and / or technological systems, obtaining unauthorized access to such resources; violation of security, sustainable, reliable and regular mode of operation of communication and / or technological systems; use of the communication system, its resources and electronic means of communication to carry out cyber attacks on other objects of cyber security.
main cyber threats in the field of education
1. Violation of confidentiality, integrity, accessibility of information stored in educational institutions and other structures of the educational system. 1.1. Hacking of databases of educators, ISUO, EDEBO, "Competition", etc. 1.2.Destruction of databases by viruses
2. Violation of security, sustainable, reliable and regular mode of operation of in-school and out-of-school document management. 2.1. Absence or inaccuracy of digital signatures. 2.2.Lack of system in the document flow.
3. Use of inaccurate, unscientific information or misinformation from the World Wide Web in preparation for or during training sessions. 3.1. Lack of criticality to e-mail information 3.2. Lack of criticality to World Wide Web sites. 3.3. Lack of criticality to information from the World Wide Web.
